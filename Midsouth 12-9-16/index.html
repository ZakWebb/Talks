Basic definitions

Quantum state $|\psi\rangle \in \mathcal{H} = \mathbb{C}^{2^n}$

  • $2$-norm of $|\psi\rangle$ is 1
  • $\langle \psi |$ is corresponding linear form

Unitary $U$

  • 2-norm preserving linear operator on $\mathcal{H}$
  • Describes evolution of quantum states

Hamiltonian $H$

  • Hermitian linear operator on $\mathcal{H}$
  • Eigenvalues correspond to energy
  • $U(t) = \exp(-i H t)$

BQP: A promise problem $(\Pi_{\text{yes}},\Pi_{\text{no}})$ is in BQP if $\exists$ a uniform family of poly-sized quantum circuits $\{\mathcal{C}_n\}_{n\in\mathbb{N}}$:

  • if $x\in \Pi_{\text{yes}}$ then $\text{Pr}\big[C_{|x|} \text{ accepts } |x\rangle\big] > \frac{2}{3}$
  • if $x\in \Pi_{\text{no}}$ then $\text{Pr}\big[C_{|x|} \text{ accepts } |x\rangle\big] < \frac{1}{3}$

QMA: A promise problem $(\Pi_{\text{yes}},\Pi_{\text{no}})$ is in QMA if $\exists$ a uniform family of poly-sized quantum circuits $\{\mathcal{C}_n\}_{n\in\mathbb{N}}$:

  • if $x\in \Pi_{\text{yes}}$ then $\exists$ $|\phi_x\rangle$, $\text{Pr}\big[C_{|x|} \text{ accepts } |x,\phi_x\rangle\big] > \frac{2}{3}$
  • if $x\in \Pi_{\text{no}}$ then $\forall$ $|\psi\rangle$, $\text{Pr}\big[C_{|x|} \text{ accepts } |x,\psi\rangle\big] < \frac{1}{3}$

Succinctly Represented Graphs!

Each vertex of $G$ corresponds to a single dimension

A $d$-degree graph $G$ is row-computable if:

  • $\exists f:V(G)\times[d]\rightarrow V(G)$
  • $f(u,i)$ gives the $i$th neighbor of $u$

We will be interested in $d$-degree, row-computable, $2^n$ vertex graphs.

$s,t$-Transport Problem

Given two vertices $s$ and $t$, does a QW take $|s\rangle$ to $|t\rangle$?

Input:

  • A $d$-degree, row-computable, $N$-vertex graph $G$
  • Two vertices $s$ and $t$
  • A total evolution time, $T$
  • Minimal probability, $p$
  • Promise on acceptance difference, $\epsilon^{-1}$

BQP-complete to determine whether:
  • $|\langle s | \exp(-i A(G) T)| t \rangle|^2 > p+\epsilon$
  • $|\langle s | \exp(-i A(G) T)| t \rangle|^2 < p$

Adjacency Matrix Smallest Eigenvalue Problem

Given a graph, what is its smallest eigenvalue?

Input:

  • A $d$-degree, row-computable, $N$-vertex graph $G$
  • Minimal energy, $a$
  • Promise on acceptance difference, $\epsilon^{-1}$

QMA-complete to determine whether:
  • $\lambda_1(A(G))< a$
  • $\lambda_1(A(G)) > a+\epsilon$
